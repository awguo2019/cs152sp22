
 
 They are not elegant, 
 the hands that have through hard labors grown robust,  
 gnarled a little at the joints, with Linked veins blue beneath parchment skin.  
 But they are good hands; powerful for their size and deft In quick and numberless services: caressing an infant's cheek or tearfully Wiping up a spill. They can mend And build but also destroy With equal ease-- these humble Hands once sculptured mountains    Or wrote Parthenons in air.
