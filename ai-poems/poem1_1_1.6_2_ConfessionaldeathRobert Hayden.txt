
 
 The death of a young black man 
 Is not an easy thing to live with 
 It doesn't matter how many times you've seen it happen 
 Or how hard you try to prepare yourself for it  
 When it comes, always unexpectedly 
 Ripping apart whatever peace you had managed to cobble together  

 You tell yourself that this is just the way things are in the world   And maybe that's true But still, each time, when it happens again    You can't help but mourn     For all the unfulfilled potential Smeared across cold concrete In ever-widening puddles Of crimson      Eventually your mind grows numb To shield itself from further hurt But even then       There are days when out of nowhere Grief will strike like lightning paralyzing your heart With its remembering
