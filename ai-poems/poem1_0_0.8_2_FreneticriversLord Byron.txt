
 
 They come rolling in like thunder,
 Rushing and roaring through the night.
 Fierce and untamed, they defy all who would try to cross them.
 
 Wild and free, they are the lifeblood of this land.
 Nourishing it with their ever-flowing waters.
 Carving out paths through mountains and valleys alike.

 In times of drought, they are a lifeline for those struggling to survive.
And in times of flood, they can bring death and destruction on an unimaginable scale.

 But we love them nonetheless; these wild rivers that run through our hearts
